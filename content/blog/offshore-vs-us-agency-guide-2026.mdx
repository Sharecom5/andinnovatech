## The Traditional US Agency Model: What You’re Actually Paying For

When you hire a boutique agency in New York, Chicago, or San Francisco, your $25k isn't just going toward the code.

**Where your money goes:**
*   **Office Rent:** Premium coworking spaces or trendy offices.
*   **Account Managers:** You pay for someone to have meetings about meetings.
*   **Sales Commissions:** Up to 20% of your bill goes to the person who sold you the project.
*   **Benefits & Local Salaries:** Senior developers in major US cities often demand $150k+. 

**The Benefit:** You can physically meet them. They understand your local cultural nuances perfectly. If they fail, you can theoretically take them to a local court.

## The Traditional Offshore Model: The "Cheap" Trap

*   **The Result:** You spend $5,000, get a broken site, and then have to pay a US firm $10,000 to fix it. This is why many US owners are (rightfully) terrified of offshore work.

## 3 Questions to Ask Before Choosing a Team

### 1. "Who is actually writing the code?"
Many US agencies quietly outsource their work anyway. If you're paying $20k for work that's being done in India or Poland without you knowing, you're just paying a massive "middleman tax." At AnD Innovatech, we are transparent about our global team.

### 2. "What happens if this breaks at 3 PM EST?"
Timezones are the biggest killer of offshore projects. Ensure the team has "overlap hours." Our team works during your business hours to ensure we are responsive when you need us most.

### 3. "Do you understand my business goals?"
A web developer is a "commodity." A **Technical Partner** is a "value-add." If the team doesn't ask how you plan to [get leads without ads](/blog/get-more-plumbing-leads-without-ads-2026), they are just pixel-pushers.
